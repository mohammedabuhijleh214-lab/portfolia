About the Warden

I'm Mohammed Abu Hijleh, known in the community as Cyber Warden — a penetration tester focused on understanding systems well enough to break them safely, and explain exactly how to fix them.

My work centers on web application security: mapping attack surfaces, chasing logic flaws, and validating the fundamentals — the OWASP Top 10, authentication and session handling, input validation — with the same rigor whether the target is a lab environment or a live engagement.

Outside of structured testing, I compete in Capture The Flag competitions with C3berus, which keeps my problem-solving sharp under time pressure and exposes me to techniques that don't show up in a typical day-to-day workflow.

Mission: build a reputation on precision, clear reporting, and continuous learning — not on shortcuts. Goal: keep pushing deeper into offensive security research, one CTF, one lab, one report at a time.
